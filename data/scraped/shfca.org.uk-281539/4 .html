  • Thursday, 20 October 2022
  • 9:00 am 5:00 pm 09:00 17:00
  • McEwan Hall (map)

Our SHFCA2022 annual conference takes place on the evening of Wednesday 19th and during Thursday 20th October, bringing together delegates with invited expert speakers to network and discuss the partnership opportunities and steps needed to build the hydrogen economy across Scotland.

SHFCA2021 Scaling up Hydrogen

  • Wed, 13 Oct 2021 1:30 pm 13:30 Thu, 14 Oct 2021 1:00 pm 13:00
  • SHFCA2021 Online (map)
  • Google Calendar ICS

Don’t miss SHFCA2021 on 13-14 October, and take advantage of the full opportunities to hear expert speakers over both days. All of the SHFCA2021 online sessions are free to attend live thanks to the support from our SHFCA2021 partners.

The theme for our 13th annual SHFCA conference is Scaling up Hydrogen for Net Zero which reflects the urgent need to tackle carbon emissions and climate change. The Scottish Government’s Hydrogen Policy Statement of December 2020 set a target for 5GW of low carbon hydrogen production in Scotland by 2030, and our SHFCA2021 plenary speakers will highlight the importance of partnerships and taking a whole energy system approach to help deliver Scotland’s 2030 targets. The three conference sessions on early market demand, hydrogen production, and enabling the hydrogen infrastructure will show progress now being made across Scotland towards Net Zero.

Hydrogen Bridges Across the North Sea

  • Thursday, 2 September 2021
  • 2:30 pm 4:00 pm 14:30 16:00
  • Scottish Hydrogen & Fuel Cell Association (map)
  • Google Calendar ICS

This webinar on Hydrogen Bridges across the North Sea Region explores how North Sea Ports will act as hydrogen bridges. Through their proximity to renewable energy and potential in the production, storage and transportation of hydrogen, ports will fulfil a key role in the energy transition to hydrogen across the North Sea Region. Speakers include Jan Egbertsen from the Port of Amsterdam, Joanne Allday from the Port of Cromarty Firth, and Paul Turner of CMB TECH. The event will be moderated by Nigel Holmes of SHFCA and Jon Jordan of the North Sea Hydrogen Ports and Maritime Community (NS HyMaP). Please register here: https://event.webinarjam.com/register/44/kokw4u28
