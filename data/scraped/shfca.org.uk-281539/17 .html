SHFCA2022 Hydrogen Scotland conference

  • Thursday, 20 October 2022
  • 9:00 am 5:00 pm 09:00 17:00
  • McEwan Hall Teviot Place, Bristo Square Edinburgh, EH8 9AG Scotland (map)
  • Google Calendar ICS

Our 14th SHFCA2022 annual conference takes place on Wednesday 19th October 2022 and Thursday 20th October as an in-person event at the prestigious McEwan Hall in Edinburgh.

SHFCA2022 kicks off on the evening of Wednesday 19th October with all delegates invited to join the giant networking reception being held in the SHFCA2022 Exhibition Zone at the prestigious McEwan Hall in Edinburgh. The main SHFCA2022 Hydrogen Scotland conference takes place on Thursday 20th October, with the First Minister of Scotland invited to give the opening keynote in the morning plenary session.

The SHFCA2022 conference programme brings together SHFCA members and guests with invited expert speakers who will highlight the partnership opportunities and steps needed to build the hydrogen economy across Scotland. The Hydrogen Scotland conference themes include partnerships for growth, sharing lessons learned across the hydrogen sector, and building up local hydrogen demand alongside the wider hydrogen export opportunities. Speakers will highlight opportunities with hydrogen and fuel cells as an enabler for energy system transition, and showcase exemplar projects and related activities that are building core skills and expertise.

Our 14th SHFCA annual conference takes place at a pivotal moment in the growing hydrogen economy, with an increasing number of projects in Scotland being announced. Hydrogen Scotland will address the importance of building partnerships and sharing lessons learned as we scale up production of hydrogen to deliver Scotland’s target of 5GW low carbon hydrogen production by 2030. Speakers will highlight opportunities for development of local supply chains and skills, together with the distributed production of hydrogen as a key part of Just Transition.

The development of Scottish onshore and offshore wind capacity will enable production of green hydrogen at multi-GW scale. Scotland has access to 25% of Europe’s offshore wind potential, with Scotland’s ultimate offshore wind potential estimated at over 500GW. The recent ScotWind leasing round allocated offshore areas capable of an additional 25GW of offshore wind generation by the 2030’s, with further significant growth expected during the late 2030’s and 2040’s along with increased production of green hydrogen to meet the ambition of 25GW installed hydrogen capacity by 2045.

SHFCA2022 Hydrogen Scotland conference will also address thornier issues, including the ‘chicken and egg’ challenge. Collaboration to build the early demand for hydrogen across Scotland will be essential to create the market pull. This demand will underpin the development of local hydrogen production together the associated infrastructure including transportation with vehicle refuelling, and networks with building and industry heat.

The recently refurbished McEwan Hall venue has capacity for over 400 delegates in the main auditorium. The SHFCA2022 Exhibition Zone is located in the McEwan Hall downstairs foyer areas which are used for serving coffee/teas during breaks and food at lunchtime.
