Let’s create a better way to search the internet

We want to make searching the internet open and transparent. Our goal is to provide a wide range of independent and free options for navigating the web. This is relevant for all organizations and people who want to boost Europe’s digital sovereignty, a greater variety of search engines, and independent search results. We can make this possible by cooperating with existing data centres and by using open source principles and public moderation. We may not be able to do it as individuals, but we can do it together.

„The Internet was meant to be free.”

The Internet is so successful because it’s open and has the potential to be developed and evolve. We can all surf it to better understand the world, conduct research and communicate with each other. But this neutrality is under threat because exactly what we uncover on the web is dictated by a few powerful search engines.

These search engines work neither openly nor transparently. And they’ve become so powerful, they decide what information they give us and how they use our data. Over ninety percent of all search queries in Europe run on a single search engine! And the same is true in many other parts of the world.

Such power, in the hands of a few commercial or state-run companies, is an invitation to abuse and manipulation.

We want to change that by bringing more diversity to Internet search, so that we continue to work, learn, research and live freely on the web of the future.

Why Open Search?

Dr. Stefan Voigt, geographer, researcher at the German Aerospace Center (DLR) and initiator of the Open Search Foundation:

“The algorithms and huge data pools of the major search engines are now commercially operated ‘black boxes’. We can’t look inside. We don’t know what companies are doing with our data. And we can’t judge which filters lead to the results that are presented to us.
That’s why we set up the Open Search Foundation. Just as road maps and land survey information are now public goods and accessible free of charge to all people, the Internet must also be mapped openly. Orientation on the Internet must become a public, transparent and freely available right.”

What can I do?

Getting smart

Learn more about search engines, privacy, network monopolies and data abuse. The Open-Search-Blog provides you with a great starting point. It’s where we present sources, specialist info and links to articles. Several times a year, the osf newsletter also provides an overview of the activities of the Open Search Initiative.

Talk about it

Make others aware of the issue. Spread the message to friends. Ask politicians to address the issue and strengthen the Open Search movement.

Actively promote

Support the Open Search Foundation actively through donations or become a sponsoring member. We also offer individual partnerships to companies and scientific institutions.

Open Search Foundation e.V.

The Open Search Foundation e.V. is a European movement of people and organisations that work together to create the foundation for independent, free and self-determined access to information on the Internet. In cooperation with research institutions, computer centres and other partners, we’re committed to a searching the web in a way that benefits everyone. The promotion of research in the field of search engines, plus education and cooperation, form the pillars of our work.
