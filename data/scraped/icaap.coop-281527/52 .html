ICA-AP PRESIDENT’S MESSAGE


This year on 2nd July, cooperatives worldwide are celebrating the 100th International Day of Cooperatives. This year also marks a decade of the United Nations (UN) International Year of Cooperatives. The UN General Assembly (2011) declared 2012 as the International Year of Cooperatives (IYC) highlighting the contribution of cooperatives to socio-economic development, particularly their impact on poverty reduction, employment generation and social integration.

With the theme “Cooperative Enterprises Build a Better World”, the IYC encouraged the growth and establishment of cooperatives all over the world. When the capitalist model failed, the IYC reminded the world that there is more than one way of doing business and that in a global economy we all have to work together. Time and again cooperatives have come together during difficult economic times to leverage their resources and showed resilience during the crisis. Today, the international cooperative movement comprises of 1 billion cooperators from 3 million cooperatives around the world. It provides jobs to 280 million people worldwide (10% of the world’s employed population). As quoted in the latest World Cooperative Monitor (2021), the Top 300 cooperatives have achieved a turnover of 2.18 trillion USD. From the Asia and Pacific region, a total of 46 cooperatives are featured in the Top 300 rankings. Cooperatives have risen to challenges and have showcased that their human-centred business model, inspired by cooperative values and principles, builds a better world.

I would like to call upon cooperators to come together in furthering the spirit of cooperation worldwide, attracting and involving more youth and women in the cooperative movement, facilitating trade among cooperatives, increasing cooperation among cooperatives and contributing to building a better world.

ICA-AP President, Dr. Chandrapal Singh Yadav



ICA-AP REGIONAL DIRECTOR’S MESSAGE


On July 2, cooperatives around the world will celebrate the 100th International Day of Cooperatives (IDC, #CoopsDay). This year’s theme, ‘Cooperatives Build a Better World,’ echoes that of the International Year of Cooperatives (IYC) in 2012. During that time, the world was coming out of the financial turbulence of 2008 and cooperatives were seen to provide some hope and clarity of direction for citizens around the world. In 2022, the world is again rapidly veering towards recession, reeling from high prices of essential commodities because of war and pandemic, and facing the prospect of millions regressing into poverty. The importance of cooperatives has been recognized across countries and is reflected in the National Constitution, strategy documents to enhance contribution to GDP, creation of new Ministries, and in the implementation of Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Cooperatives need to once again step up and show how they are building a better world by bring economic resources under democratic control, creating ownership of resources, providing employment opportunities, building resilience, protecting the environment, and creating social capital.

In the ten years between 2012 and 2022, cooperatives have progressed at various levels on the implementation of the two Blueprints. Their work has been recognized in the Voluntary National Reviews on the implementation of SDGs in several countries. In 2016, UNESCO added cooperatives to the list of intangible cultural assets. Cooperatives were described as entities that “allow for community building through shared interests and values creating innovative solutions to societal problems. The 2021 World Cooperative Monitor documents the role of cooperatives in addressing needs of communities during the pandemic and mentions that, “promoted the potential for action by cooperatives in the recovery phase to spread an economic model that is more sustainable and more inclusive. Participation and attention to members and a community focus are key points that have guided cooperatives.” Most recently, the resolutions and conclusions (June 2022) from the ILO conference (110th session) on Decent Work and the Social and Solidarity Economy explicitly mentions cooperatives in the definition of SSEs.

“Cooperatives are answering the wake-up call of UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres, who warned that the world is ‘on the edge of an abyss — and moving in the wrong direction’, and exclaimed that ‘to restore trust, and inspire hope, we need cooperation, we need dialogue, we need understanding’. For nearly two centuries, cooperatives have been pulling in this direction. Free market rewards competing, dominating, and elbowing; but what we need is empathy, solidarity or concern for the public good. Market and private gain don’t account for social stability, health, or happiness. To rebuild battered economies and to make it better for the community and environment; cooperatives need to show the way to inclusive, resilient and sustainable economies and societies that respect people and planet.

ICA-AP Regional Director, Mr. Balasubramanian Iyer

Member Activties



National Cooperative Union of India (NCUI) is jointly celebrating the 100th International Day of Cooperatives with the Ministry of Cooperation, Government of India on July 4, 2022 in New Delhi, India. The theme of the event is ‘Cooperatives Build an Aatmanirbhar Bharat (Self-reliant India) and a Better World’.

National Cooperative Federation (NCF) of Nepal invites all the Nepalese cooperators to celebrate the 100th International Day of Cooperatives and 29th National Cooperative Federation Day on July 2, 2022 at the NCF Seminar Hall, Lalitpur, Nepal.

Japan Co-operative Alliance (JCA) and Cooperatives in Japan will jointly hold the 100th International Day of Co-operatives Forum in hybrid mode on July 4, 2022, in Tokyo! In the forum, they will share the best practices of Japanese Cooperatives and explore the co-operative identity to celebrate the 100th International Day of Co-operatives. The forum will be held in Japanese and invite Japanese co-operators nationwide.
